## Limitations & Caveats
As an artist, I must acknowledge the "white space"—what this table leaves out. While the Brazilian representation is lush, there is a noticeable thinness in electronic or avant-garde "glitch" art (save perhaps for the Philip Glass Ensemble at the very end). The "Contemporary" era is well-represented, but the "traces we leave behind" (the historical context) are heavily weighted toward Western classical music. There is a lack of ancestral or indigenous sonic textures which, to me, represents a "blind spot" in the archive’s palette. 

Furthermore, the lack of "medium" data (is this vinyl? digital? live recording?) means I have to infer the "texture" of the sound based on the name alone. I am painting with my eyes closed to the technical fidelity, relying instead on the "feeling" these names evoke in the cultural zeitgeist.
