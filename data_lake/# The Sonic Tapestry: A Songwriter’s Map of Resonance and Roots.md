## Trends & Patterns

As I scan from ID 1 to ID 275, there is a clear trend in how creators identify themselves. We start with the short, punchy monikers of the rock era—*Accept (2), Aerosmith (3), Queen (51)*. These are brands, designed to be spray-painted on a wall. As we move into the classical section, the names become long, descriptive, and institutional—*English Concert & Trevor Pinnock (208)* or *Academy of St. Martin in the Fields, John Birch, Sir Neville Marriner & Sylvia McNair (222)*. 

**My Interpretation**: It shows a transition from the "Cult of Personality" in rock music to the "Cult of the Composition" in classical music. In my world, we try to find a middle ground—a name that means something, but music that stands on its own.

## Limitations & Caveats

It represents the "Winners"—those who made it into the catalog.
