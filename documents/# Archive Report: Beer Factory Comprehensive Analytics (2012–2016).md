# Archive Report: Beer Factory Comprehensive Analytics (2012–2016)

**Authorized by:** The Root Beer Brand Database  
**Record Scope:** Global Root Beer Brand Registry, Transactional History, and Consumer Review Metrics

## 1. Brand Lineage and Market Hierarchy

Accessing historical records confirms that the root beer market is anchored by deep-seated heritage. Among all brands within my records, **Barq’s** is identified as the oldest, holding the longest history in the database. In contrast, **River City** is the brand with the shortest brewed history. 

My analysis of the market’s economic structure reveals significant variance in profitability and market share. **A&W** is identified as the root beer brand with the lowest unit profit available to wholesalers. Despite this narrow margin, larger conglomerates dominate the volume; in 2014, **AJ Stephans Beverages** accounted for approximately 4.945% of all root beers sold, meaning fewer than one in twenty root beers were produced by them. By 2016, the **Dr Pepper Snapple Group** emerged as the brewery associated with the highest purchase count. Furthermore, Dr Pepper Snapple Group maintains the highest number of purchases among brands that do not utilize Facebook or Twitter for advertising.

## 2. Global Distribution and Operational Infrastructure

The physical infrastructure supporting these brands is concentrated primarily in the Northern Hemisphere. I have 23 breweries located in North America within my dataset, while **Australia** is represented by exactly one brewery. 

Operational data for the Sacramento region highlights two primary distribution nodes: **Sac State Union** and **Sac State American River Courtyard**. 
*   **Sac State Union:** This location recorded a total of 3,216 transactions and holds the record for the highest number of bottled root beers sold. Notably, it outperformed the Courtyard in sales of **Dog n Suds** root beer.
*   **Sac State American River Courtyard:** This location accounted for approximately 49.05% of all sales. In 2015, this node moved 131 sweet, bottled root beers that did not contain cane sugar. 

For logistical tracking, I record high consumption of **Thomas Kemper** at latitude 38.559615, where 2,717 bottled units were sold.

## 4. Product Composition and Quality Indices

The database tracks the intersection of ingredients and consumer satisfaction. A primary trend is the preference for traditional sweeteners; there were 3,255 more cane sugar root beers sold than corn syrup root beers. Interestingly, brands containing honey sell about 4.38 fewer units per day on average than those without. I have identified exactly one brand that is available in cans and contains both corn syrup and artificial sweeteners.

Quality is measured through customer reviews. **Jayne Collins** holds the record for the highest number of reviews, though she gave her lowest rating to **Henry Weinhard’s**. The average star rating across all subscribers is 3.11.

**Performance by Brand:**
*   **Blue Dog Beverages:** The brewery associated with the brand holding the highest count of 5-star ratings.
*   **Captain Eli’s:** Led all brands in 5-star ratings during the 2012 period.
*   **River City:** While it received 28.0303% 5-star ratings, it is also the brand that has been worst rated the most times, holding the maximum count of 1-star ratings (BrandID 10018).
*   **Gale’s Bread and Butter:** Produced the top-selling root beer of 2015.
*   **Elite Ratings:** Only six brands—**IBC, River City, Frostie, Sprecher, Thomas Kemper, and Virgil’s**—met the threshold of receiving 5-star ratings from at least five different customers.

## 5. Temporal Sales Observations

Specific periods show distinct fluctuations. In August 2014, exactly two **Bulldog** root beers were purchased, while **A&W** recorded zero sales for that entire month. However, by 2016, 14 canned A&W units were moved. My data also tracks the introduction of brands; between 1996 and 2000, the registry added **Captain Eli’s**, then **Bulldog**, and finally **Sparky’s Fresh Draft**. 

On average, 1.2511 caffeinated root beers are sold per day. For premium bottled products priced above $2.00, the mean purchase price is exactly $3.00.
