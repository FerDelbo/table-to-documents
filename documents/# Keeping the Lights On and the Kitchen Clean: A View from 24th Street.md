# Keeping the Lights On and the Kitchen Clean: A View from 24th Street

By Maria Rodriguez, Owner of La Casita Bakery

Running a bakery in the heart of the Mission isn't just about the smell of fresh pan dulce or making sure the coffee is hot by 6:00 AM. For twelve years, I’ve carried my parents’ legacy in these flour-dusted hands, and that means taking the "business" side as seriously as the baking. 

Lately, I’ve been spending my Sunday evenings—my only real time to breathe—looking into how we, as a city, are doing when it comes to food safety. I know, I know. For most of us, a surprise health inspection is the stuff of nightmares. But when you look at the records for San Francisco, you start to see the bigger picture of what we're all up against.

### The Scale of Our Community
We aren't alone in this. In the broader state records, there are 5,902 restaurant owners in California fighting the same fight we are every day. Right here in San Francisco, the city was incredibly busy in 2016; I noticed that 2,758 distinct restaurants were inspected that year alone. Across those visits, inspectors recorded 3,881 routine inspections. 

It’s a massive undertaking. And while it feels like they’re out to get us sometimes, there is a "Gold Standard" we’re all chasing. Across the board, 2,087 restaurants managed to hit that perfect inspection score of 100. It’s a small number compared to the thousands of us operating, but it shows that perfection is possible if you’re meticulous.

### Learning from the "Cautionary Tales"
I’ve always said that if you want to stay sharp, you have to look at where others stumble. Even the big names have their days. Take **Peet's Coffee & Tea**—they actually had the highest number of inspections among all establishments. Back in 2014, they also recorded the highest number of low-risk violations. Despite that, they managed an average score of about 94.95, which isn't easy to maintain when you have that much volume.

Then you have places like **Tiramisu Kitchen**. I was looking at their history—they’ve had three routine inspections, all scoring above 70, with an overall average of 89.33. But it just takes one bad day. On January 14, 2014, they were cited for two low-risk violations. Over time, they actually ended up with three high-risk violations total. It just goes to show that even a solid B-plus average can hide some serious underlying risks.

We also have to watch the "paperwork" side of things. I remember seeing that **Evergreen Garden Restaurant** was actually the first business to ever get a low-risk citation for something as simple as not having their permit or inspection report posted. It’s a reminder to me: keep those frames on the wall clean and visible!

### High Stakes and High Risks
The reality of our city is tough. There are 4,205 high-risk violations associated with San Francisco restaurants in the records. Some names really stand out for the wrong reasons—**Specialty's Cafe & Bakery** unfortunately holds the record for the highest total number of high-risk violations among everyone evaluated. 

And then there's the extreme end of the scale. We all worry about our scores dipping, but **ABC Bakery Cafe** holds the record for the lowest inspection score ever recorded. On the other hand, a place like **KABABAYAN FAST FOOD** had a particularly rough day on September 26, 2016, recording the lowest score for an unscheduled routine inspection that day.

It’s not just about the "mom and pop" shops either. Even **Whole Foods Market** isn’t immune—they actually had the highest number of inspections initiated by customer complaints. It reminds me that our customers are our real inspectors; they see everything we do.

### A Neighborhood Perspective (94110)
Being here in the 94110 zip code, I pay close attention to my neighbors. I noticed an interesting pattern: among businesses in our area that have inspection scores below 95, about 56% of those violations are classified as "Low Risk." To me, that suggests that most of us are doing the big things right—keeping the fridge at 40 degrees and the pests out—but we’re getting tripped up on the smaller maintenance details.

### Why We Do It "By the Book"
It’s easy to complain about the 6,774 low-risk violations recorded city-wide back in 2014, or the 36,344 violations that came out of unscheduled inspections in 2016. It feels like a lot of red tape. But then I see a stat that stops me cold: in 2014, there were 30 foodborne illness investigations conducted. That’s 30 times someone potentially got very sick from a meal that was supposed to be a treat.

That is why I stay on my team about the details. Whether it's the 74 businesses in 94117 classified as High Risk, or the fact that even a place like **Soma Restaurant And Bar** had to go through three unscheduled routine inspections, it shows that the city is watching.

I’m proud of my 12 years here. I’m proud that I know the tax codes like H25 (which 1,299 other businesses share) and H24 (like **Rue Lepic** uses). It might seem like just numbers to some, but to me, it's the language of staying in business. 

We’re all in this together—from the 28 owners who operate five or more establishments to the single owner-operator like me. Let's keep the scores high and the risks low. Our community deserves nothing less.

— *Maria*
