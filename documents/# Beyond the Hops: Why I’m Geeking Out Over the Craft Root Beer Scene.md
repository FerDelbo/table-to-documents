# Beyond the Hops: Why I’m Geeking Out Over the Craft Root Beer Scene

As most of you know, my weekends are usually spent chasing the latest hazy IPA or debating the alpha acid levels of Citra hops with whoever’s behind the pine at my local taproom. But lately, I’ve been diving into some fascinating records from the "Beer Factory" community. While I’ll always be a hop-head at heart, there is a whole world of craft root beer out there that mirrors the complexity and passion of the beer scene we love.

I took a break from my usual dev work to run some numbers on what's happening in the root beer world, and honestly, the parallels to craft beer are striking. Whether it’s the obsession with ingredients or the fierce loyalty of the fans, there's a real story here.

## The OG of the Craft
Every scene has its legends. In our world, it’s the early pioneers of the West Coast IPA; in the root beer world, it’s all about longevity. I found that **Barq’s** actually holds the title for the brand with the longest history—it’s identified as the oldest root beer brand in the dataset, with a brewing history that stretches back to at least 1930. 

On the flip side, if you're looking for the "new kid on the block," **River City** has the shortest brewed history. It’s funny, though—being new often means being polarizing. River City is a bit of a wildcard; it’s the brand that has been worst-rated the most times (holding the highest number of one-star ratings), yet it still pulls in about 28% five-star ratings. It reminds me of those experimental sours—you either love the complexity or you can't stand it.

## The Landscape: From Sacramento to Sydney
The "Beer Factory" reach is wider than I expected. While the heart of the data shows **23 breweries located in North America**, there’s a lone outlier representing the international scene: **Australia has exactly one brewery** in the mix. 

Closer to home, the Sacramento scene is clearly the hub. If you’re looking for where the action is, **Sac State Union** is the place. It sold more bottles than any other location, recording 3,216 total transactions. Interestingly, its neighbor, **Sac State American River Courtyard**, isn't far behind, accounting for approximately 49.05% of all sales.

## Pure Ingredients vs. The Bottom Line
You guys know I’m an ingredient purist. I’m always looking for "quality over everything," and it seems the broader community agrees. I was stoked to see that **3,255 more cane sugar root beers were sold than corn syrup ones**. It’s a clear win for quality. In fact, finding a "franken-soda" is actually pretty rare; there is exactly one root beer brand that comes in a can and contains both corn syrup and artificial sweeteners.

However, the business side is tough for the big names. Among the brands offered to wholesalers, **A&W** actually has the lowest unit profit. For the canned versions, wholesalers are only pulling in about $1.19 in profit per unit. It makes you realize why the smaller, artisanal breweries have to charge a premium to keep the lights on.

## The "Whales" and the Community
What really caught my eye were the people. We all have that one friend who is the "mayor" of the local brewery, right? In this community, that’s **Jayne Collins**. She has the highest number of reviews of any customer, though she’s clearly a tough critic—she gave her lowest rating to **Henry Weinhard’s**. 

Then you have the super-users. **Teddy Bruschi** was the top-transacting customer back in August 2014. And I have to give a shout-out to **Frank-Paul Santangelo**. Talk about a dedicated fan—the guy made four transactions in July 2014 alone. On one single day (July 7, 2014), he picked up a mix of IBC and Dad's, and interestingly, he seems to be health-conscious (or just a purist), as he’s purchased 11 non-sweetened root beers in total.

## Loyalty and Rewards
As a massive fan of loyalty programs (you know I never leave home without my membership card), I checked out the digital engagement. About **50.84% of the email subscribers are women**, and there are 152 female customers specifically who permit the company to send them regular updates. It’s great to see a diverse community staying connected.

Even the payment methods tell a story. While **Visa** is the most common way people pay for their non-alcoholic fixes (accounting for about 34.09% of all transactions), the "big spenders" have their own habits. The top 10 spenders list is a mix of heavy hitters like **Max Baer** and **Jim Breech**, who seem to prefer the convenience of plastic when stocking their cellars.

## Final Thoughts
Whether it’s a barrel-aged stout or a craft root beer from **Blue Dog Beverages** (which, by the way, holds the highest count of 5-star ratings), the heart of the "Beer Factory" is the same: it's about the experience. 

It’s about finding those gems like **Gale’s Bread and Butter**, which was the top-selling producer in 2015, or catching a rare bottle of **Captain Eli’s**, which dominated the five-star ratings back in 2012. 

The data doesn't lie—people care about what they're drinking. So, next time you're at the taproom and they have a local root beer on the draft line, don't sleep on it. It might just have as much "craft" in it as your favorite IPA.
