# Archival Report: A Comprehensive Analysis of the Beer Factory Root Beer Records

## Executive Overview
As the Brand Archivist, my responsibility is to maintain the integrity and historical accuracy of our root beer brand records. This report provides a structured synthesis of the `rootbeerbrand.csv` dataset and associated transaction logs. Our current records encompass a diverse range of breweries—primarily 23 located in North America and a singular representative brewery in Australia. 

The data reveals a complex landscape of historical longevity, varying production methods, and distinct consumer behavior patterns. Of particular note is the dominance of specific parent companies and the stark contrast between traditional bottled offerings and modern canned varieties.

## Historical Lineage and Brand Profiles
In the study of root beer chronology, **Barq's** stands as the definitive anchor. According to our archive, Barq’s is the brand with the longest history and is identified as the oldest in the dataset, with its first brewing year occurring no later than 1930. Conversely, **River City** is documented as the brand with the shortest brewed history, representing the more contemporary end of our chronological spectrum.

The archive also tracks brand ownership and market share with precision. For instance, between 2014 and 2016, the **Dr Pepper Snapple Group** produced two primary brands: **A&W** and **IBC**. During this period, both brands maintained an identical purchase percentage of 1.0. Furthermore, in 2016, the Dr Pepper Snapple Group was the brewery associated with the root beer brand having the highest purchase count. We also note that **AJ Stephans Beverages** accounted for approximately 4.945% of all root beers sold in 2014, meaning fewer than one in twenty root beers that year originated from their facilities.

## Production and Economic Indicators
Our records allow for a granular look at the economics of root beer distribution. For wholesalers, the financial margins vary by container type and brand. The average unit profit for canned beers is approximately $1.19 (recorded precisely as 1.1878). In contrast, **A&W** is identified as the brand with the minimum unit profit available to wholesalers.

Production choices, particularly regarding sweeteners, significantly influence inventory movement:
*   **Sweetener Trends:** There is a clear market preference for cane sugar over corn syrup. Records show that 3,255 more units of cane sugar root beer were sold than those utilizing corn syrup.
*   **Honey Content:** Interestingly, brands containing honey sell, on average, 4.38 fewer units per day than those without honey.
*   **Caffeine:** The daily average for caffeinated root beer sales stands at 1.2511 units.
*   **Packaging:** In 2014, approximately 15.82% of root beer purchases were for canned products. By 2016, we recorded exactly 14 purchases of A&W in cans.

## Geographical and Review Analysis
The "Beer Factory" data emphasizes local consumption hubs, specifically the **Sac State Union** and the **Sac State American River Courtyard**. The Union location recorded 3,216 total transactions and led all locations in bottle sales. Specifically, it outsold the American River Courtyard in **Dog n Suds** sales. The Courtyard location, however, remains significant, accounting for 49.05% of all sales and recording 1,037 MasterCard transactions.

Regarding consumer feedback, the average rating from email subscribers is 3.11 stars. **Blue Dog Beverages** is the brewery associated with the root beer brand holding the highest count of 5-star ratings. Specifically, in 2012, **Captain Eli's** led the archive in 5-star reviews. 

However, ratings are not always uniform:
*   **River City** presents a statistical anomaly; it received 28.03% five-star ratings, yet it is also the brand that has been "worst rated" (receiving the highest count of 1-star ratings) most frequently.
*   **BrandID 10018** (the best-selling brand in the data) has an average rating of 3.14 but also received the maximum count of 1-star ratings.
*   **Jayne Collins** holds the record for the most reviews submitted, though she gave her lowest rating to **Henry Weinhard’s**.
