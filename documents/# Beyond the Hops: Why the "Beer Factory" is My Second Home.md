# Beyond the Hops: Why the "Beer Factory" is My Second Home
*By Alex Miller*

I’ve always said that a great brew is about more than just the liquid in the glass; it’s about the story, the craft, and the community that gathers around the tap. As someone who spends a good chunk of my time (and my graphic design freelance checks) at the local taproom, I’ve become a bit of a student of the "Beer Factory" scene. Whether I’m grabbing a flight of something experimental or sticking to a classic, I’m always looking at the details.

Recently, I’ve been digging into the history and the habits that make our local beer culture what it is. It turns out, there’s a lot more going on under the surface than just a well-carbonated pour.

## A Legacy of Craft
I’ve always had a soft spot for the trailblazers. If you look at the heritage of what’s being served, **Barq’s** stands out as the elder statesman. It’s the brand with the longest history in the books, with its first brewing year going back no later than 1930. That’s the kind of longevity I respect. 

It’s also fascinating to see how the "local" label expands. While I love supporting my neighbors, the reach of this factory is impressive—there are **23 breweries located across North America** feeding into this ecosystem, and we even have a lone connection all the way in **Australia**. 

## The Regulars and the Reviewers
You start to recognize the names when you hang out at the taproom enough. I’m a regular, but I’ve got nothing on **Teddy Bruschi**, who was the absolute top-transacting customer back in August 2014. Then there’s **Frank-Paul Santangelo**, another July regular who made four separate trips that month. I actually saw the records of one of his runs on July 7th—he walked out with a mix of **IBC** and **Dad’s**. Interestingly, Frank-Paul seems to have a specific palate; he’s picked up **11 non-sweetened root beers** in his time.

And let’s talk about the critics. We all have opinions, but **Jayne Collins** is officially the voice of the people with the most reviews of anyone. She’s tough, though—she gave her lowest rating to **Henry Weinhard’s**. On the flip side, people like **James House** keep it concise with three solid reviews. 

I’m also seeing a real shift in how we pay and stay connected. More than half of the folks on our email list (about **50.84%**) are women, which I love—craft beer shouldn't be a boys' club. And while I’m a "taproom first" kind of guy, it’s clear the **Sac State Union** is the hub of the community, recording **3,216 transactions** and moving more bottles than any other location.

## The Tasting Notes: Sugar, Honey, and Cans
As a designer, I’m always looking at the packaging, and as a connoisseur, I’m looking at the ingredients. There’s a massive divide in what we’re drinking: we’ve sold **3,255 more root beers made with cane sugar** than those made with corn syrup. To me, that’s a win for quality. Interestingly, the brands that use honey actually sell about **4.38 fewer units per day** on average than the non-honey ones. Maybe the sweetness profile is a bit too niche for the daily crowd?

When it comes to the "Gold Standard"—the five-star ratings—a few names consistently rise to the top. **Blue Dog Beverages** is the brewery that’s captured the most five-star hearts, but you’ll also see **IBC, River City, Frostie, Sprecher, Thomas Kemper, and Virgil’s** hitting that threshold. **River City** is actually a fascinating case study; while about **28% of its ratings are five stars**, it’s also the brand that has been "worst rated" the most times. It’s bold, and clearly, it’s not for everyone.

## The Logistics of the Pour
I sometimes worry about my favorites going out of stock, especially since I know the wholesalers are working on thin margins for some brands. **A&W**, for example, has the lowest unit profit for wholesalers. It’s a classic, but maybe that’s why it’s harder to find in short runs. 

I’ve also noticed the container shift. In 2014, fewer than **16% of our root beers were sold in cans**, but by 2016, we were seeing things like **A&W in cans** picking up steam. Personally, I’m a bottle guy—there’s something about the weight of it. Apparently, **Jim Breech** agrees with me; he’s picked up **23 bottles** himself. 

## Final Thoughts
Whether it’s **Captain Eli’s** leading the pack for five-star ratings (like it did back in 2012) or the **Dr Pepper Snapple Group** dominating the 2016 sales, the "Beer Factory" is a living, breathing thing. It’s more than just data to me; it’s the guy next to me at the bar using his **Visa** (the most common card here, by the way) to try a new **Thomas Kemper**. 

It’s about the **1.25 caffeinated root beers** sold every day keeping the students at the **Sac State American River Courtyard** going. It's about the craft. And as long as they keep brewing, I'll keep showing up to see what’s new on tap.

*Alex Miller is a graphic designer and a member of the Beer Factory Loyalty Program. You can usually find him at the Sacramento taproom on Friday afternoons.*
