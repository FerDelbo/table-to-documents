# The App Store State of the Union: The Good, The Bad, and the Totally Abandoned

By Alex, *App Store Power User & Reviewer*

I’ve spent more time in the App Store than I care to admit. Between hunting for productivity hacks and trying to find a game that doesn't feel like a cash grab, I’ve seen it all. But lately, I’ve been digging into the trends across the store—looking at what people are actually saying, what’s being ignored, and where the real value lies. If you’re tired of downloading duds, here is my "state of the union" on the current app ecosystem.

### The "Perfect" Club and the Popularity Paradox

Let’s start with the elite. There are exactly **274 apps** sitting with a perfect **5-star rating**. You’d think these are the world-beaters, right? Well, when you look closer, these 5-rated apps only average about **8.74 reviews** each. It’s easy to keep a clean sheet when only a handful of people are looking. Compare that to the giants we all use every day. On the App Store, the ten most reviewed apps are the ones you’d expect: **Facebook, WhatsApp Messenger, Instagram, Messenger, Clash of Clans, Clean Master, Subway Surfers, YouTube, Security Master, and Clash Royale.** 

These apps have millions of eyes on them, which means they can't hide their flaws. Take **Candy Crush Saga**, for instance. It’s one of the biggest free apps out there, but it also carries the crown for the highest number of negative comments among free apps. In fact, across all the heavy hitters with **100,000,000+ installs**, I’m seeing a staggering **5,157 negative comments**. It just goes to show: the more people use your app, the more chances you have to annoy them.

### Shopping, Photography, and Where the Vibe is Right

If you’re looking for a new way to spend your paycheck, the shopping category is dominated by five big names: **eBay, Wish, The Coupons App, Groupon, and AliExpress.** Personally, I've seen **Groupon** consistently stay in that top five for reviews, which tells me people are still hunting for those local deals.

On the flip side, if you're a shutterbug, **Google Photos** is absolutely crushing it in the Photography genre. It holds the highest total sentiment subjectivity score, meaning people have *a lot* to say about it—mostly because it’s become such a central part of how we store our lives. 

And for those of you into niche hobbies, the **Comics** category is actually one of the most consistent, maintaining an average rating of **4.1**. It seems comic fans are a bit more satisfied than your average gamer. Speaking of games, the **Racing** genre is doing surprisingly well with an average rating of **4.46**, though only about **17.67%** of those reviews are explicitly tagged with positive sentiment. People like the games, but they aren't always shouting it from the rooftops.

### The Good, the Neutral, and the "Wait, What?"

I always tell people to look at the "Neutral" reviews—that’s where the truth usually lives. For example, **HTC Weather** has a decent **3.9 rating** on the Google Play Store, but **six users** are firmly on the fence with neutral feedback. In total, weather apps across the store have about **35 neutral comments**. It’s hard to get excited about the rain, I guess.

Then you have apps like **"10 Best Foods for You"** in Health & Fitness. It’s got **44 users** who gave it a neutral shrug. Even **Dino War: Rise of Beasts** has **five neutral reviews** recorded. It’s not that the apps are bad; they just aren't hitting that "wow" factor. 

And then there are the weird ones. **Coloring book moana** is listed as a best-selling app, yet its sentiment polarity is **-0.2**. That means people are buying it, but they aren't exactly happy about it. And don't get me started on the data gaps. **Onefootball - Soccer Scores** and **Dragon Ball Legends** both have missing or null ratings in the records I’ve seen. It’s frustrating as a reviewer when you can’t even see the consensus on a popular title.

### My Biggest Beef: The Abandonment Issue

Here is a statistic that absolutely infuriates me as a user: **100% of free apps with a rating of 4.5 or higher have not been updated since 2018.** 

Read that again. The "best" free apps on the store are essentially digital ghosts. They’ve gone years without an update. As a reviewer, I hate seeing developers sit on their laurels. If an app is successful, maintain it! 

We also see some laziness in the feedback loop. About **13.56% of "no comment" reviews** come from apps rated for **Teens**. And for the lowest-rated **Mature 17+** apps? The only listed comment is often just "**nan**"—literally nothing. If you’re going to put out a "Mature" app, at least have the decency to moderate the feedback or provide a description. Interestingly, the **Adults only 18+** category is a ghost town, with only **40 total installs** recorded. One of the only reviews there is just a simple "AWESOME!! thanks." Short, sweet, and probably the only person who found it.

### Value and Pricing

If you're looking for love, it’s cheap—the average price for **Dating apps** is exactly **$0.00**. But be careful with "free." **Cooking Fever**, which targets "Everyone," has a slightly negative sentiment score (**-0.04**), likely due to those pesky in-app purchases. 

On the other end of the spectrum, the most expensive app in the dataset actually has a massive total sentiment polarity score of over **11,242**. It seems if you pay a premium, you’re either getting incredible value or you're part of a very exclusive, very happy club. 

### Final Thoughts for the Community

Before you hit "Install," do your homework. Look past the 5-star rating if there are only eight reviews. Check if the app has been updated since the 2010s. If you’re looking for a safe bet, **Google Photos** or a high-rated **Racing** game are usually solid. If you’re looking at **FREEDOME VPN**, just know that only **12.5%** of the recorded sentiment is positive—maybe keep looking.

I’ll keep writing these reviews and digging into the stats because developers need to know we’re paying attention. Whether it’s a **6.6M** utility like **Browser 4G** (which surprisingly has **20 users** feeling pretty positive about it) or a giant like **Messenger**, we deserve apps that work and developers who care.

Stay savvy, 

**Alex**
