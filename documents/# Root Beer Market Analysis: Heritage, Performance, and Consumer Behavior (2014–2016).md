# Root Beer Market Analysis: Heritage, Performance, and Consumer Behavior (2014–2016)

**By: Arthur "Artie" Birch, Root Beer Brand Analyst**

As a lifelong student of the non-alcoholic beverage sector, I have spent decades curating the "Beer Factory" dataset—a comprehensive registry that captures the pulse of the root beer industry. My analysis of the 2014–2016 period reveals a fascinating intersection of long-standing heritage and shifting consumer preferences. Whether we are discussing brand equity or the granular unit profit available to wholesalers, the data tells a story of a market that is as diverse as the flavor profiles it offers.

## The Heritage Spectrum: From Barq’s to River City

In the world of root beer, history is often synonymous with brand loyalty. My records confirm that **Barq’s** remains the brand with the longest history in this dataset, with its first brewing year occurring no later than 1930. It stands as the definitive elder statesman among the root beer brands identified in the Beer Factory files.

On the opposite end of the spectrum, we see the "new guard." **River City** holds the title for the brand with the shortest brewed history. However, youth does not necessarily mean lack of quality; interestingly, about 28% (28.03%) of ratings for River City are five stars. That said, it is a polarizing brew, as it has also received the highest number of one-star ratings among all brands in the set.

Looking at the expansion of the craft during the late 90s, my database shows a specific sequence of "first brews" between 1996 and 2000. Listed from the most recent to the earliest, we see **Sparky's Fresh Draft**, followed by **Bulldog**, and finally **Captain Eli's**. These brands represent the mid-market surge that helped define the modern craft soda landscape.

## Market Landscape and Brewing Geography

The geographic footprint of our industry remains heavily concentrated in North America, which is home to 23 breweries within this dataset. International representation is sparse but notable; for instance, Australia is represented by exactly one brewery. 

When we look at market dominance, the **Dr Pepper Snapple Group (DPSG)** stands out as a juggernaut. In 2016, DPSG was the brewery associated with the brand having the highest purchase count. Between 2014 and 2016, they successfully managed two of the most recognizable names in the business: **A&W** and **IBC**, both of which maintained a 1.0 purchase percentage during that window. Furthermore, DPSG leads the market among brands that do not utilize social media platforms like Facebook or Twitter for advertising.

However, dominance doesn't always equate to high margins for the middleman. My analysis of wholesale structures shows that **A&W** is actually the brand with the lowest unit profit available to wholesalers. This is a classic example of high-volume, low-margin brand positioning.

## Consumer Profiles and Transactional Trends

Our database allows for an incredible deep-dive into the "who" behind the glass. We see dedicated enthusiasts like **Jayne Collins**, who holds the record for the highest number of reviews, though she notably gave her lowest rating to **Henry Weinhard’s**. We also track high-frequency purchasers like **Frank-Paul Santangelo**. Frank-Paul is a particularly interesting case study; he made four transactions in July 2014 alone and has purchased a total of 11 non-sweetened root beers. On one specific day—July 7, 2014—he purchased both IBC and Dad's, with only one of those four total items being in a can.

Other notable "top-tier" customers include:
*   **Teddy Bruschi**: The top-transacting customer for August 2014.
*   **Natalie Dorris**: Recorded her first purchase on August 27, 2015.
*   **Jim Breech**: A clear devotee of the glass container, having purchased 23 bottles.

Payment preferences also offer insight into retail operations. **Visa** is the most commonly used credit card at Beer Factory, representing approximately 34.09% of all transactions. In 2014, we also saw a significant volume of **MasterCard** usage, with 110 transactions recorded.

## Ingredient Influence and Product Performance

The chemistry of the brew significantly impacts market performance. In 2014, the difference in sales between root beers using cane sugar versus those using corn syrup was substantial—3,255 more units of cane sugar root beer were sold. This preference is reflected in consumer satisfaction; among beers containing corn syrup, the most common rating is a mediocre 2 stars.

Furthermore, my "analyst's take" on the inclusion of honey is supported by the numbers: root beer brands containing honey sell, on average, 4.38 fewer units per day than those that do not. In a market where the average daily sale of a caffeinated root beer is only 1.25 units, these margins are critical.

## Regional Retail Snapshot: The Sac State Microsystem

The data from our two primary locations—**Sac State Union** and **Sac State American River Courtyard**—provides a perfect laboratory for retail trends. The Courtyard location accounted for nearly half of all sales (49.05%), yet the Union location led in specific categories. For example, the Sac State Union sold more **Dog n Suds** than the Courtyard and had the highest overall volume of bottled beer sales.

Specific logistical data points of interest:
*   **Total Transactions**: Sac State Union recorded 3,216 transactions.
*   **Location Ratio**: The American River Courtyard sees approximately 96.27% as many transactions as the Union.
*   **Can vs. Bottle**: While bottles dominate, 14 canned A&W were purchased in 2016. In 2014, fewer than 16% of all root beers sold were in cans.

## Conclusion

The root beer industry is a blend of nostalgia and hard-nosed economics. From the historical prestige of Barq’s to the 2015 market leadership of **Gale's Bread and Butter**, the data shows a resilient and varied market. As an analyst, I find the 3.11-star average rating from our email subscribers to be an honest reflection of the category—a solid, dependable beverage with a few "five-star" standouts like **Blue Dog Beverages**, which currently holds the highest count of top-tier ratings. As we move forward, the brands that balance flavor integrity with smart wholesale pricing will be the ones to watch.
