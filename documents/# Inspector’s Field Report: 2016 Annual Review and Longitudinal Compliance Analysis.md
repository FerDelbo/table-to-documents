# Inspector’s Field Report: 2016 Annual Review and Longitudinal Compliance Analysis

**Prepared by:** David Chen, Public Health Inspector  
**Date:** January 15, 2017  
**Subject:** Summary of Inspection Activities, Risk Assessment, and Business Compliance Trends

---

### **Overview**

As we close out the records for the previous calendar year, I have conducted a thorough review of our inspection data to assess the state of food safety within our jurisdiction. My primary objective remains the prevention of foodborne illness through rigorous enforcement of the health code. In 2016, our department was particularly active; we recorded 3,881 routine inspections across the city. During that period, 2,758 distinct San Francisco restaurants underwent inspection, providing us with a comprehensive snapshot of the industry’s current health standards.

While my daily work involves on-site verification, the aggregate data allows us to identify high-risk patterns that require our immediate attention. My commitment to the community is to ensure every "Full Service Restaurant," "Bakery," and "Mobile Food Facility" operates within the bounds of safety.

---

### **The Gold Standard: Excellence in Compliance**

It is important to acknowledge the establishments that view the health code not as a hurdle, but as a standard of excellence. My records indicate that a total of 2,087 restaurants met all inspection requirements during their evaluations, achieving a perfect inspection score of 100. This is a significant achievement. Historically, this level of excellence fluctuates; for instance, in 2013, we saw 184 unique eateries attain the maximum inspection score. 

Furthermore, in high-density areas like ZIP code 94102, I noted that 128 distinct businesses met or exceeded the 90+ score threshold during 2015. This demonstrates that even in high-traffic urban environments, maintaining a sanitary facility is entirely achievable.

---

### **High-Risk Violations and Priority Enforcement**

Despite these successes, we continue to face challenges with high-risk violations that pose a direct threat to public health. In San Francisco alone, restaurants are associated with a total of 4,205 high-risk violations. 

When I prioritize my inspection route, I focus on establishments that show a pattern of critical failure. According to the data, **Specialty's Cafe & Bakery** currently ranks first for the total count of high-risk violations among all evaluated restaurants. No other entity in our records exceeds their count in this specific, critical category. Similarly, **Business ID 75139** has accumulated more total violations than any other business in our history, despite being inspected 702 times. When a business requires that much oversight, it places a significant strain on our departmental resources.

Other notable enforcement data points include:
*   **ABC Bakery Cafe** holds the record for the all-time lowest inspection score.
*   **KABABAYAN FAST FOOD** recorded the lowest score during the routine unscheduled inspections conducted on September 26, 2016.
*   On October 4, 2016, **Stacks Restaurant** was documented with six distinct types of violations in a single visit. 

I must also note that in 2016, San Francisco recorded the highest number of establishments flagged as high risk for health and safety compared to other cities in the region. This validates my systematic approach to unannounced inspections.

---

### **Establishment Profile: Tiramisu Kitchen**

I have been monitoring **Tiramisu Kitchen** closely over several cycles. To date, the count of routine inspections for Tiramisu Kitchen stands at three. Their performance has been relatively stable, with an average inspection score of approximately 89.33 across all visits. Notably, all three of those routine inspections resulted in scores above 70.

However, a closer look at their 2014-01-14 inspection shows two low-risk violations. Furthermore, they have incurred three high-risk violations over their history. When comparing them to other facilities, such as the **OMNI S.F. Hotel - 2nd Floor Pantry**, Tiramisu Kitchen has recorded a higher count of low-risk violations. It is my role to educate the owners here on how to bridge the gap between "satisfactory" and "excellent."

---

### **Trends in Unscheduled and Complaint-Driven Inspections**

The efficacy of our department often relies on the element of surprise. In 2016, we saw 36,344 violations associated specifically with unscheduled inspections. Some businesses, like **Soma Restaurant And Bar**, have undergone three such unscheduled routine visits to ensure ongoing compliance.

We also respond heavily to public concerns. **Whole Foods Market** currently holds the record for the highest number of inspections initiated by complaints. It is essential that we address these community-reported issues immediately, especially considering that in 2014 alone, we conducted 30 formal Foodborne Illness Investigations.

---

### **Administrative and Regulatory Findings**

Accuracy in our records is paramount. Currently, I am tracking 5,902 restaurant-owner records in California. Our database shows that 28 owners meet the threshold of operating five or more establishments. Interestingly, the owner with the highest establishment count maintains an impressive average inspection score of approximately 97.57 across their portfolio.

We also monitor administrative compliance, such as the posting of permits. **Evergreen Garden Restaurant** was the first business I cited for a low-risk violation regarding a "Permit license or inspection report not posted." On a broader scale, 2014 saw a total of 6,774 violations categorized as low-risk across the city.

Other geographical and administrative notes from my files:
*   There is exactly one eatery located in **Hayward**.
*   In the 94117 ZIP code, 74 unique businesses are currently categorized as high-risk.
*   **Peet's Coffee & Tea** maintains the highest number of inspections in our records, with an average score per inspection of 94.95. However, in 2014, they also recorded the highest number of low-risk violations.
*   Establishments like **Rue Lepic** (Tax Code H24) and those located at **1825 Post St #223** (a single-business address) remain under regular surveillance.

---

### **Conclusion**

The data from 2016 underscores a fundamental truth in public health: consistency is key. While the 34 distinct eateries classified as low-risk despite having "unpermitted food facility" descriptions are a concern I am currently rectifying, the 2,087 perfect scores give me hope. 

My systematic approach—verifying the 1,299 businesses under tax code H25 or investigating why 56% of businesses scoring below 95 near ZIP 94110 fall into the low-risk category—will continue. We are here to ensure that whether a citizen is eating at a high-volume chain or a small bakery, the food they consume is safe.
