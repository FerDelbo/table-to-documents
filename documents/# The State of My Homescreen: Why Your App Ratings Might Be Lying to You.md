# The State of My Homescreen: Why Your App Ratings Might Be Lying to You

Look, I’ve downloaded hundreds of apps over the years. I’m the person who actually reads the changelog before hitting "Update" and the one who leaves a detailed three-paragraph review when an app starts crashing on my morning commute. I want things that work, things that are stable, and things that actually deliver on their store page promises.

Lately, I’ve been digging into what’s actually happening in the App Store, and honestly? Some of the trends I'm seeing would make any pragmatic user a bit skeptical. Here is my "Alex-style" breakdown of what’s going on in the ecosystem right now.

### The "Five-Star" Myth and the Big Players
We all see those perfect ratings and think, "This is it, the perfect tool." But did you know there are exactly 274 apps in the store with a perfect 5-star rating? That sounds great until you realize that these 5-rated apps only average about 8.74 reviews each. It’s easy to be "perfect" when only a handful of people have talked about you. 

When you look at the heavy hitters—the ones we actually use every day—the story changes. The most reviewed apps are exactly who you’d expect: Facebook, WhatsApp, Instagram, and Messenger. In fact, if you’re looking for the top free installs, you’re looking at Google Play Books, Messenger, WhatsApp, Google Chrome, and Gmail. These are the giants, but even they aren't immune to the "noise" of the community.

### The Stale App Problem (My Biggest Pet Peeve)
I’ve said it before and I’ll say it again: I cannot recommend an app that is being ignored by its developers. Here is a stat that absolutely blew my mind: **100% of the free apps with a rating of 4.5 or higher haven't been updated since 2018.** 

Every. Single. One. 

As a user who cares about stability, that’s a massive red flag. A 4.5 rating from six years ago is meaningless if the app hasn't been optimized for modern operating systems. It’s a ghost town of "good" software that’s one OS update away from breaking forever.

### Sentiment vs. Reality: It's Complicated
I always tell people to read the actual reviews, not just look at the stars. Take **Coloring book moana**, for example. It’s a best-seller, yet its sentiment polarity is sitting at -0.2. That means people are buying it, but they aren't necessarily happy once they open it. 

The same goes for **Cooking Fever**. It’s rated for "Everyone," but it has a negative average sentiment score of about -0.04. On the flip side, you have something like **Golf GPS Rangefinder: Golf Pad**. It’s sitting at a solid 4.6 on Google Play with a positive sentiment score (around 0.27). That’s the kind of app I’m looking for—one where the math matches the vibe.

### Genre Deep Dives: Where to Spend Your Time
If you're browsing specific categories, here’s what I’ve noticed:
*   **Photography:** If you care about how others feel about their photos, **Google Photos** is the king of subjectivity. It ranks first in total sentiment subjectivity within the Photography genre; no other app even comes close.
*   **Shopping:** Looking for deals? The most reviewed shopping apps are eBay, Wish, The Coupons App, Groupon, and AliExpress. If millions of people are talking about them, they’re usually doing something right (or something very wrong).
*   **Dating:** Interestingly, the average price for dating apps is $0.00. We’re all looking for love for free, it seems.
*   **Racing:** This is actually a very high-performing genre, with an average rating of 4.46 across the board. However, only about 17.6% of the sentiment-labeled reviews are actually positive, so there's clearly some frustration under the hood.

### The "Neutral" Crowd and Missing Data
Sometimes apps just don't evoke a strong reaction. I noticed **HTC Weather** has a 3.9 rating, but there are six users who are completely neutral about it. Across all weather apps, I found about 35 of these neutral comments. It's the same for the **10 Best Foods for You** app in Health and Fitness; 44 users couldn't decide if they loved it or hated it. Even the action game **Dino War: Rise of Beasts** has 5 neutral reviews sitting there.

Then there are the apps that are just... mysteries. **Onefootball - Soccer Scores** has no recorded rating or sentiment score in the current data. **Dragon Ball Legends** is also missing a rating, though interestingly, not a single user is recorded as disliking it yet. And then there's **Honkai Impact 3rd**—a massive Action game, but its highest sentiment score is currently "nan." 

### Final Thoughts for Devs and Users
Devs, if you're listening: fix the "stale" problem. We see when you stop updating. To my fellow users: keep being vocal. Whether it's the **FREEDOME VPN** (where only 12.5% of sentiments are positive) or **Basketball Stars** (which has over 10 million downloads but some very low sentiment scores from users who "dislike it pretty much"), your feedback matters.

Don't just look at the 100,000,000+ install count. Remember that in that top-tier install category alone, there are over 5,157 negative comments. Numbers are big, but the user experience is what actually counts.

Now, if you'll excuse me, I have a few more 2018-era apps I need to go uninstall.
