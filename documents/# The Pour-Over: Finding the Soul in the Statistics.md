# The Pour-Over: Finding the Soul in the Statistics

There’s something about walking into a taproom that just feels like home. Being a graphic designer here in Portland, I’m probably biased toward anything with a well-designed label and a local story, but it’s more than just the aesthetics. It’s the community. When I’m not at my desk, you’ll usually find me at the brewery, flight in hand, trying to deconstruct the latest small-batch release. Lately, I’ve been diving deep into the "Beer Factory" scene, and honestly, the stories hiding behind the bar are just as complex as a double-hopped IPA.

### The Roots of the Craft
Most people think "craft" is a new-school movement, but the history goes back further than you’d think. Take root beer, for example—the gateway brew for many of us. If you look at the heritage of what’s on tap, **Barq's stands out as the root beer brand with the longest history**; it’s officially the oldest in the dataset. It’s wild to think about that longevity compared to something like **River City, which actually has the shortest brewed history** among the brands we follow. 

We’re lucky to live in a hub of innovation. There are **23 breweries located in North America** within our community network, though I’ve always been fascinated by that **one lone brewery representing Australia** in the mix. It really shows how global the appreciation for a good brew has become.

### The Legends of the Taproom
Every brewery has its "regulars"—the folks who define the culture. If we were giving out a loyalty award for July 2014, it would have to go to **Frank-Paul Santangelo, who made four transactions** that month alone. I love looking at his haul from July 7, 2014; he grabbed **root beers from both IBC and Dad's**, and interestingly, **only one of those four purchases was in a can**. He’s clearly a purist, having also purchased **11 non-sweetened root beers** over time.

But Frank isn't the only heavy hitter. In August 2014, **Teddy Bruschi took the title for the most transactions**, while **Jayne Collins holds the crown for the most reviews overall**. I respect that hustle; as someone who values the "Gold" tier perks, I know how much work goes into actually documenting your palate. Jayne is a tough critic, though—she gave her **lowest rating to Henry Weinhard's**. On the other hand, **James House has contributed three reviews**, which is a solid start for any enthusiast.

### Where the Magic Happens
I’m a total sucker for the taproom experience, and the numbers show I’m not alone. Looking at the local hubs, the **Sac State Union recorded a total of 3,216 transactions**, and it turns out they **sold more bottles of beer than any other location**. They’ve also got the lead on Dog n Suds sales compared to the American River Courtyard. 

Speaking of the **Sac State American River Courtyard, it still holds its own, accounting for about 49.05% of all sales**. It’s where **1,037 MasterCard transactions** went down, and in 2015, people there bought **131 sweet bottled root beers that didn't use cane sugar**. Personally, I’m a cane sugar snob—and it looks like I have company. Across the board, there were **3,255 more cane sugar root beers sold than corn syrup versions**. That’s the kind of quality control I like to see.

### Trends, Tastes, and Tidbits
What’s trending? Well, **Blue Dog Beverages is currently the brewery associated with the brand holding the most five-star ratings**. If you’re looking for a crowd-pleaser, that’s your mark. However, if you're looking for the "best-seller" that everyone loves to debate, look at **BrandID 10018**. It’s the **best-selling root beer**, but funny enough, it also **received the maximum count of 1-star ratings**. It’s the polarizing "love it or hate it" brew of the factory!

A few other things I found fascinating:
*   **The Purists**: Between 2014 and 2016, the **Dr Pepper Snapple Group produced two mainstays: A&W and IBC**. Both had a 1.0 purchase percentage for that period.
*   **The Canners**: While I prefer glass, 14 canned A&W root beers were purchased in 2016. Generally, **wholesalers are seeing about a $1.19 profit per unit on canned beers**.
*   **The Tech-Savvy**: We’re a Visa-heavy crowd. **Visa is the most common card for non-alcoholic purchases**, representing about **34.09% of all recorded transactions**. Even in Folsom, we’ve got **56 customers who prefer to pay with Visa**.
*   **The Hidden Gems**: **Gale's Bread and Butter brewed the top-selling root beer of 2015**, and **Captain Eli's was the 5-star king back in 2012**.

### The Final Sip
It’s easy to get lost in the spreadsheets, but for me, this is about the people and the craft. Whether it’s **Peg Winchester exploring Fitz's and Bundaberg**, or **Tom Hanks stopping by for two root beers between 2015 and 2016**, every transaction is a story. 

I’m always looking for that next five-star experience—like the ones found with **Sprecher, Thomas Kemper, or Virgil’s**, which are among the elite few to receive at least five 5-star ratings from different customers. As for me? I’ll keep following the new releases, staying on that email list with the other **152 female subscribers** who care about the "what’s next," and hoping I don't miss the next limited run. 

See you at the taproom—I'll be the one at the corner table, probably over-analyzing the label art. Cheers!
