# Internal Memorandum: 2016 Year-End Food Business Registry & Inspection Oversight Summary

**To:** Director of Public Health, Food Safety Division  
**From:** Office of the Municipal Food Business Registrar  
**Date:** October 24, 2023  
**Subject:** Comprehensive Review of Registry Integrity and Inspection Data (2012–2016)

## 1. Executive Overview

As the custodian of the city’s official food business registry, it is my responsibility to ensure the accuracy and accessibility of all data concerning food establishments within our jurisdiction. This report provides a formal synthesis of our records, focusing on registration volumes, inspection performance, and violation trends recorded through the 2016 calendar year. 

Our records currently reflect a robust food service landscape. Within the broader scope of our data, we track 5,902 restaurant owners across the state of California. However, my primary focus remains on the integrity of our local San Francisco files. In 2016 alone, our division oversaw the inspection of 2,758 distinct San Francisco restaurants, conducting a total of 3,881 routine inspections during that period. 

## 2. Registry Composition and Growth

The registry has seen consistent activity since the 2012 reporting cycle. In 2012, we processed applications for 473 new eateries, signaling the beginning of a significant expansion in the local food sector. 

Our database categorization remains precise, utilizing tax codes and postal identifiers to maintain order. For instance, our records show 1,299 businesses currently assigned tax code H25. In contrast, businesses like "Rue Lepic" operate under tax code H24, under which they have undergone both unscheduled routine inspections and follow-up reinspections. 

Geographic precision is paramount to this office. We have verified that exactly one eatery is registered in the Hayward area within our specific dataset. Within San Francisco, we maintain highly granular data; for example, we can confirm that only a single business is officially recorded at the address 1825 POST St #223.

## 3. Inspection Standards and Compliance Excellence

The Department maintains a rigorous scoring system where a 100-point score represents perfect compliance with all health requirements. I am pleased to report that 2,087 restaurants have achieved this perfect score. Historically, in 2013, we saw 184 unique eateries attain this maximum rating.

However, compliance is not uniform. Our records identify two establishments with inspection scores falling below the 50-point threshold. The establishment known as ABC Bakery Cafe currently holds the record for the all-time lowest inspection score within our registry. Furthermore, during unscheduled routine inspections, the business Chairman Bao has maintained a concerningly low average score of approximately 0.0321.

## 4. Violation Analysis and Risk Management

A significant portion of my duties involves the meticulous logging of violations. These are categorized by risk level to assist health inspectors in prioritization.

### High-Risk Violations
In San Francisco, we have recorded a total of 4,205 high-risk violations. My records indicate that Specialty's Cafe & Bakery currently ranks first for the highest total count of these critical infractions. On a specific operational level, Tiramisu Kitchen has incurred three high-risk violations over its history. It is also worth noting that in 2016, San Francisco recorded the highest number of establishments flagged for significant health and safety hazards compared to other cities in our dataset.

### Low-Risk and Procedural Violations
Low-risk violations, while less urgent, are a key indicator of overall management quality. In 2014, we documented 6,774 low-risk violations across the city. Peet's Coffee & Tea was noted for having the highest count of these lower-tier infractions during that year. 

Specific instances of procedural failures are also tracked. For example, Evergreen Garden Restaurant holds the distinction of being the first business cited for failing to post a permit license or inspection report. In the 94110 area, among businesses scoring below 95, approximately 56% of their recorded violations fall into the low-risk category.

## 5. Case Studies in Registry Persistence

To illustrate the depth of our records, I have selected a few entities for detailed review based on their interaction frequency with this office:

*   **Tiramisu Kitchen:** Our files show this establishment has undergone three routine inspections, maintaining an average score of 89.33. On January 14, 2014, their inspection resulted in two low-risk violations. Notably, Tiramisu Kitchen has historically recorded more low-risk violations than the OMNI S.F. Hotel - 2nd Floor Pantry.
*   **Peet’s Coffee & Tea:** This entity has the highest number of total inspections recorded in our registry, maintaining a respectable average score of 94.95 despite the volume of oversight.
*   **Whole Foods Market:** This establishment is notable for having the highest number of inspections initiated specifically by public complaints.
*   **Soma Restaurant And Bar:** Our records confirm three unscheduled routine inspections for this location.
*   **Business ID 75139:** This specific identifier is associated with the highest cumulative violation count in our registry, despite the entity having undergone 702 inspections.

## 6. Special Investigations and Administrative Actions

Our registry also tracks specialized inspection types. In 2014, we facilitated 30 distinct foodborne illness investigations. We also monitor the activity of high-volume owners; currently, 28 owners in our registry operate five or more establishments. Interestingly, the owner with the most establishments maintains a high standard of safety, with an average inspection score of approximately 97.57 across their portfolio.

We also note specific geographical clusters of risk. In the 94117 postal code, there are 74 unique businesses currently classified within the high-risk category. Conversely, some successes are highly localized; in 2015, the 94102 postal code saw 128 distinct businesses achieve inspection scores of 90 or higher.

## 7. Statement of Data Limitations

As the Registrar, I must emphasize that the information provided herein is a reflection of the official records as of the last entry. While we provide the geographic coordinates and primary contact information—such as the business at 428 11th St having the most low-risk violations—we do not provide real-time operational status or subjective quality assessments. 

This office remains committed to the procedural integrity of the `businesses.csv` dataset and will continue to provide authoritative data to support the Department’s mission of public safety.
