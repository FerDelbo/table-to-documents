## I. Geographical and Topographical Specifications

A primary function of this archive is the maintenance of precise coordinates and topographical data. According to our records, the **Silverstone Circuit** maintains a unique geographical distinction: among common European fixtures like the Hockenheimring and the Hungaroring, Silverstone lies at the highest latitude, situated farther north than its German and Hungarian counterparts.

From a topographical perspective, altitude remains a critical variable for aerodynamic and engine performance calculations. The record for the Formula 1 circuit with the maximum elevation identifies **Malaysia** as the host country for this high-altitude venue. Conversely, our records indicate that the average lap record time across all circuits located in **Italy** stands at 90,064.80 milliseconds (approximately 1 minute and 30.06 seconds). 

For those seeking further public-domain verification, the database links the **Red Bull Racing** entry—the constructor with the highest points in Race No. 9—to its authoritative reference at [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing).

The database serves as the definitive timeline for the evolution of the Grand Prix calendar. Of particular note:
