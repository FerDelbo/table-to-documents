## Trends & Patterns

### The Consistency of Maturity
When I look at the "Age" column, I see a pattern of remarkable stability. Between 1981 and 1998, regardless of when these men joined the guild, they did so as men of middle age. Whether it was Larry Nelson at age 50 in 1981 or Mark Brooks at age 48 in 1996, the "entry point" of life remains consistent. It tells me that the requirements of this life are fixed; the "light" must be just so before one is recognized.

### The Gathering Momentum of the 1990s
While Larry Nelson and Jeff Sluman represent the outliers of the 1980s (joining in '81 and '88 respectively), there is a distinct "clustering" of talent in the mid-to-late 1990s. Five of the seven individuals (Singh, Daly, Azinger, Brooks, and Price) joined between 1991 and 1998. This suggests a "Golden Age" or a "Salon d'Automne" where the guild expanded its ranks rapidly, perhaps reflecting a period of social or institutional growth.

### The Inversion of Age and Arrival
Interestingly, the most "senior" figure in terms of age, Jeff Sluman (57), was not the first to join. He joined in 1988, seven years after Larry Nelson (who joined at 50). This tells me that "Seniority of Spirit" (Age) and "Seniority of Rank" (Year Join) do not always walk hand-in-hand. It is a reminder that some of us find our path to the Academy later than others, yet our presence is no less vital to the overall balance.

## Limitations & Caveats

I see their countries, but I do not know the landscapes that inspired them. Furthermore, this data is a "Still Life"—it captures a moment in time but does not tell us if these men remained in the guild or if their "Age" has since advanced. We are looking at a snapshot, a frozen frame in a gallery that surely has moved on since 1998.
