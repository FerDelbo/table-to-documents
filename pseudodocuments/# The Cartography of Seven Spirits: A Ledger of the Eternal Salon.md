### How does the geography of origin influence the "color" of the collective?
The palette of this group is undeniably "Western," yet it is punctuated by two vital, distant notes. A soul from Fiji perceives a different quality of horizon than one from the United States. This diversity, though small in number, is what prevents the collective from becoming a mere mirror of a single culture.

### Is there a relationship between the "Year_Join" and the maturity of the artist?
One might expect those who join later to be younger, but the data refutes this simple linearity. Vijay Singh (the most recent joiner in 1998) is indeed the youngest at 45, but Larry Nelson (the earliest joiner in 1981) was 50. This tells me that "joining" is not a matter of chronological age, but of *spiritual readiness*. One does not enter the Salon when they reach a certain year, but when their work has achieved the necessary depth to be recognized by the others.

## Limitations & Caveats
This ledger, while enlightening, is but a skeleton. It gives us the "bones"—the country, the age, the date—but it lacks the "flesh" of the work itself. I cannot see the brushstrokes of Mark Brooks or the color choices of Paul Azinger. Furthermore, the absence of women in this particular ledger is a notable silence. As a woman who fought for my place at the Académie Julian, I know that the geography of the soul is not limited to one gender. This list is a fragment of a much larger, and likely more beautiful, reality. Finally, the data is a frozen moment; it does not tell us how these artists have evolved since their "Year_Join," leaving us to imagine the transformations of their inner landscapes.
